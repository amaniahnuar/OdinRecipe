Megi Goreng

Description

A classic in Malaysia especially when going to "mamak" restaurant spot. This is usually made using Nestle's Maggi brand Mi Kari instant noodles and possible the best to replicate the exact taste since this is what is being used nationwide. However, (as much as I don't recommend it) it is posisble to use any "curry" or in Malaysia "kari" based instant noodles. Bear in mind, this is a recipe for fried noodles so the base of the soup, or the "soup broth" powder very important for the taste; hence why this specific Maggi brand noodle is always used nationwide.

Ingredients

  • 200g instant noodles (Megi)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 100g protein of your choice (fried tofu cubes, shrimp, or chicken)
  • 2 tablespoons chili paste or sambal
  • 1 cup sliced cabbage
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 spring onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • 2 eggs
  • Salt to taste
  • Lime wedges for garnish
  • Fried shallots for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare instant noodles (Megi) by boiling them in water for 2-3 minutes or until cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a wok or frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Saut√© minced garlic and sliced onions until fragrant.
  4. Add protein of your choice (fried tofu cubes, shrimp, or chicken) and stir-fry until cooked.
  5. Add chili paste or sambal for spiciness and stir well.
  6. Mix in vegetables like sliced cabbage, bean sprouts, or chopped spring onions. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes.
  7. Season with soy sauce, sweet soy sauce, and a little salt or sugar to taste.
  8. Add the cooked instant noodles to the pan and toss everything together until well coated and heated through.
  9. Push the noodles to one side of the pan and crack an egg on the empty side. Scramble it lightly, then mix it into the noodles.
  10. Serve hot, garnished with lime wedges and fried shallots for added flavor.
